Detailed Description

Graphene electronic fluid class in two dimensions.

The DiracGraphene2D class describes a fluid governed by the usual continuity and Cauchy momemtum equations, where the mass of the fluid element is not constant. It overrides class Fluid2D necessary methods in order to describe the semi-classical electronic fluid.

◆ CflCondition()

void DiracGraphene2D::CflCondition ( )
overridevirtual

Calculates \(\Delta x\) and imposes Courant–Friedrichs–Lewy condition to \(\Delta t\).

The method retrieves the spatial discretization \(\Delta x = L / N_x \) and then sets the time step as \(\Delta t = \Delta x/\lambda \) where \(\lambda\) is given by

\begin{*eqnarray} \lambda =1.2v_F \quad&\mathrm{if}\quad S<0.36v_F\\ \lambda =1.97S + 0.5 v_F \quad&\mathrm{otherwise} \end{*eqnarray}

◆ SetSimulationTime()

void DiracGraphene2D::SetSimulationTime ( )
overridevirtual

Sets the total simulation time in units of \(v_0/L\).

The method predicts and set the maximum time for a typical Dyakonov-Shur instability simulation such that:

  1. It is larger than the usual saturation time
  2. After the saturation at least 10 oscillations occur

Our tests concluded heuristically that such criteria can be met setting \(T_{max}=5+0.02S+20/S\)

◆ WriteFluidFile()

void DiracGraphene2D::WriteFluidFile ( float  t)
overridevirtual

Writes the line of time t on the simplified .dat file output.

As a way to easily access the main results of the simulation the simplified .dat file stores the following quantities by columns:

  1. Time \(t\)
  2. Density at drain contact for electrons \(n(x=L)\)
  3. Mass flux along x at drain contact for electrons \(p_x(x=L)\)
  4. Density at source contact for electrons \(n_x(x=0) \)
  5. Mass flux along x at source contact for electrons \(p_x(x=0) \)
  6. Density at drain contact for holes \(n(x=L)\)
  7. Mass flux along x at drain contact for holes \(p_x(x=L)\)
  8. Density at source contact for holes \(n_x(x=0) \)
  9. Mass flux along x at source contact for holes \(p_x(x=0) \)
